SPECIALIZED CLARIFICATION OF DYSPLASTIC CHANGES

The two most common female cancers include breast cancer and cervical cancer. The early detection of these two types of cancer is one of the most important tasks in our practice. The Cancer smear (PAP test), vaginal palpation, secretion diagnosis, vaginal and, if necessary, abdominal Ultrasound of the abdomen, as well as palpation of the breast (with referral for mammography if necessary) are the basis of every gynecological examination.

PAP-Test:

This smear is used for the early detection of cervical cancer. For this purpose, first of all one Mirror adjustment of the cervix and then the removal of examination material from the Cervical surface and from the cervical canal. The test material is analyzed in the laboratory within 3-4 weeks and divided into 5 groups (PAP, I-PAP, V) scaled. You will be notified by us if the cancer smear shows abnormalities or if a therapy or further clarification is required. In the case of certain PAP findings, supplementary HPV diagnostics may be necessary. make sense.

HPV-Diagnostic:

Human papillomaviruses (HPV) are among the most common sexually transmitted infections in the Genital. Certain types of HPV play a role in the development of cervical cancer and other genital diseases. Cancers play a crucial role; other types may include genital warts, burning, or Provoke intermenstrual bleeding. Many adults become infected with HPV in the course of their lives - however, this infection usually has no consequences, because the immune system can successfully fight it. The HPV test is used to extend the screening and is currently a private service. He is recommended for all women over the age of 30 every 3 years and costs € 100.
